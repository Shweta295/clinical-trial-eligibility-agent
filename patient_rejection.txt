ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
Ms. Vasquez is a 62-year-old woman with newly diagnosed Stage IIIA (T3N2M0) squamous cell NSCLC, EGFR/ALK wild-type, PD-L1 TPS 62%, ECOG PS 1, deemed unresectable by tumor board, with excellent organ function and no prior systemic therapy for her lung cancer. She meets the majority of inclusion criteria and does not have most common exclusionary conditions.

However, I want to flag the following concern for the screening team: Ms. Vasquez received adjuvant pembrolizumab (an anti-PD-1 immune checkpoint inhibitor) for a prior Stage IIB melanoma, with her last infusion approximately 7 months ago. The protocol states that "no prior systemic therapy for NSCLC is allowed" and permits prior adjuvant/neoadjuvant chemotherapy if completed >12 months ago — but pembrolizumab is not chemotherapy, it is an immunotherapy agent. Additionally, NVB-7734 targets the PD-L2/TIGIT axis, which is mechanistically related to the PD-1 pathway that pembrolizumab targets. I defer to the study team and medical monitor regarding whether this prior anti-PD-1 exposure is disqualifying.

The prior melanoma itself is in complete remission with no recurrence in over 2 years, so it likely falls under the exception for "malignancy considered cured with minimal risk of recurrence."
